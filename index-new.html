Learning From Complex Cases

Diagnostic Reasoning in Hospital Medicine

Case Presentation: 82-Year-Old Male with Recurrent Pneumonia

A teaching case demonstrating the importance of comprehensive chart review, diagnostic reasoning, and care coordination

Learning Objectives

  • Develop systematic approaches to comprehensive chart review
  • Recognize infection risk in chronic lymphoproliferative disorders
  • Apply clinical reasoning when laboratory and clinical findings diverge
  • Implement antibiotic stewardship principles in complex patients
  • Identify system-level opportunities for quality improvement

Note: This is a real case with identifying information removed. The focus is on learning and systems improvement, not individual performance.

Initial Presentation

Chief Complaint

"Altered mental status, breathing problem"

First Admission (Day -8)
  • Neighbor found patient confused and short of breath
  • Symptoms present for several days
  • Patient unable to provide reliable history (dementia)
  • Chest X-ray: Bibasilar opacities
  • Started IV antibiotics, clinical improvement
Hospital Day 5
  • Left Against Medical Advice
  • Was alert and oriented × 3, had capacity
  • Family wanted long-term care placement
  • Patient refused: "I was tired of being in the hospital"
  • Discharged on oral antibiotics
48 Hours Later
  • Re-presentation to Outside Hospital ED
  • Neighbors found patient somnolent
  • SpO2 84% on room air
  • Initially hypotensive (BP 65/45)
  • Transferred to our facility for higher level of care

Key Context: Patient lives alone, progressive dementia, medication non-compliance, still driving despite cognitive impairment
